8 years in the making

How We Got Here

It started over eight years ago. Two strangers at Endeavour, grinding through MBA prep, sharing cafes and conversations that always lasted longer than they should have. You were someone I genuinely loved spending time with — your laugh, the way you'd look at me when I said something stupid, how effortlessly everything felt when you were around.

Honestly? I liked you. A lot. More than I ever let on.

But life had its own plans. We took different paths. Different cities. Different journeys. And somewhere along the way, we became friends — the kind that stays connected through late-night calls, random check-ins, and the comfort of knowing someone out there truly gets you.

We navigated life together from a distance. Our heartbreaks, our wins, our messy middles. We cheered each other on as we achieved things. You were always there. And I was always there for you. Through all of it.

Eight years. And time and again your thought would cross my mind and I would shrug it off thinking it's never gonna be.

Then you told me about Canada, 2 years ago. And something inside me broke a little. I thought — if she leaves, that's it. I might not ever see her again. I need to see her one more time. Maybe the last time. I wasn't sure. So I flew to Bangalore.

I wanted to tell you everything. How much I liked you. How much I'd always liked you. But you were leaving. And I didn't want to make it harder. I just wanted to spend as much time with you as I possibly could. So I stayed — four days at your place. We partied together, cooked together, laughed until 3am. And I loved every single moment of it.

When it was time to say goodbye, I made a choice. I told myself — let her go with happiness, not with the weight of your feelings. I accepted being just a friend.

And I let you go.

I thought that was the end of our story. That the universe had decided we were meant to be friends and nothing more.

But then you came back.

And the moment I heard you were gonna be in India again, I couldn't think of anything else. I had to see you. I needed to see you. Eight years of holding back, and I just couldn't do it anymore.

September 30, 2025 — Delhi

The Day Everything Changed

There you were. Standing at Delhi Station. Rushing all the way to come and pick me up. My heart was racing before I even stepped off the train. I had this quiet hope burning inside me — maybe, just maybe, this time could be different.

And then — the auto ride. You sat next to me, and our hands found each other. No rehearsed lines, no grand gesture. Just your fingers laced through mine, and suddenly eight years of waiting didn't matter anymore. The whole city disappeared.

We went to CP. We laughed like we always do. We talked like no time had ever passed. And somewhere between the lights and the noise and you looking at me with those eyes — I knew. I was done running from this.

So I braved it. I asked if I could kiss you.

And that kiss? That kiss sealed everything. Eight years of "what ifs." Every time I almost said something but didn't. Every goodbye that felt too heavy. Every late-night call where I wished you were next to me instead of on the other end of a phone. In that one moment — it all made sense.

I always knew — if you took one step towards me, I'd take two. And Neha, you didn't just take a step.

You ran straight into my heart. 💕

And then that night, I told you I'd been thinking about marriage. About looking at profiles. About settling down. And somehow — god knows how — that conversation turned into everything I'd been holding inside for eight years.

I told you how much I liked you. How I'd always liked you. How I could see a future with you — a real one. And you know what? You said it back. You opened your heart too. And for the first time in eight years, we were finally, completely honest with each other.

And here we are.

Two people in different countries, different continents — and yet closer than we've ever been. Planning our trip to Japan to meet again. Counting days across time zones. Building something real out of eight years of almost.

Never in my life have I taken anything this seriously, Neha. Not my career, not my goals, not anything. You are the one thing I'm sure about. There is nothing — nothing — I would change about how we got here.

I love you. More than words on a screen could ever say.

I want us to be together. I want you in my life — not just as someone I call at 2am, not just as my favourite person, but as my partner. My better half. My wifey.

Eight years. Two cities. One ocean. A hundred almost-moments. And it all led here — to you and me, exactly where we were always meant to be.
